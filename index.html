Selected Works 2017—2018

01

Experimental Posters


Design has always been a conscious, well thought out process. There must always be an underlying rationale behind the work we produce. But what if we didn't have to? What if we were to allow the subconscious mind to partake in the design process? By silencing the conscious mind, we let the unknown take control, turning flesh and bones into an automaton. This experiment aims to explore the creative potential of the implicit cognition. Prompted by the writings of E.E Cummings and T.S Eliot, the automated designer begins to release the creativity of the subconscious mind. Thus, discovering new possibilities.


Dimensions: 297mm X 420mm

02

[Untitled Archive]


Done as a gift for my family, [Untitled Archive] features a series of photographs I took during a family trip to Taiwan. The intention was to preserve the memories, and to make the ambience of the trip tangible. As the condition of the weather was rainy and foggy, it left us feeling inevitably listless. This led to a slightly melancholic and poetic approach for the book.

The key part of the book is its chapter openers. The objective was to allow readers to experience the fog we faced throughout the trip. To execute the idea, a translucent paper stock is used for all chapter openers, allowing the contents of the page beneath to be visible. However, as the reader flips the page, the contents gradually disppear.


Dimensions:
175mm X 240mm

03

Working Out Your Stress


We were tasked to design a visual brief based on the topic of city stress. Upon our research, we decided to come up with a brief that helps tackle the stress we face at work and how it affects our well-being. This visual brief is set in black and white to reflect the monotonous routine of our daily work lives. The yellow highlighters are not only an emphasis on certain keywords but they are also an indication that the situation needs to be changed for the better.

04

Typology: A Classification of Typefaces


Typology is a book that features a classification of the different typefaces. Atop the main book sits a smaller publication which is a specimen for 'Albertus'. The concept for Typology is a combination of 'structure' and 'playfulness'. The sophisticated layout builds the structure, while the design-related riddles, that are used to showcase the typefaces, contribute to the 'playfulness'. The royal blue color also sits in between the spectrum of dull and vibrant, balancing that equilibrium.


Dimensions:
200mm X 290mm

05

STIR


STIR is a fictional Design Conference which explores the correlation between graphic design and psychology. The task was to design a 'provaction' for it. Using the brain and a link cursor as semiotics, it implements the effect that design has on the human mind.

The brochure maintains the same concept. Although, it features different semiotics where arrow cursors are seen poking at the human gut instead. This digital distortion is also applied to the typography to build a stronger and more distinctive identity for the conference.


Dimensions: 594mm x 841mm (Posters)
148mm x 210mm (Brochure)

06

Vernacular Culture and its Aesthetics


This is a collaborative project done in conjunction with Adeline Ang. As part of our final year project, we were exploring the aesthetics and value of Vernacular design. Instead of rejecting what Modernist designers would classify as 'bad design', this dossier talks about how 'bad design' can be a positive influence to designers of today. Thus, recreating the 'ugly' into something beautiful.

07

Panic! Zine


A zine which shares not only the historical origin of the word 'panic' but also how it has been used over the years until today. The zine is intended to defy design rules, and to let loose of its structure, in order to exhibit the 'lack of control' we face during a state of panic.

175mm X 240mm
